May 10th, 2017 - Baby Got Front
Hello again!

Demo day for the October cohort of The Coding Boot Camp at UT Austin is in the books, which means I'm officially done with the program!  It was a lot of fun, I learned a ton, and I met some great people, many of whom I see as peers and intend to keep in touch with.

The boot camp's end, of course, marks a change in strategy for me.  Instead of remaining a "sponge", without any particular direction outside of the curriculum of the course, I am now compelled to select a specialization.  Thankfully, I've done so, and that specialization is admittedly still pretty general, though less so than before.  To begin my career, I will work as a front-end developer.


While the boot camp was advertised as a full-stack program, the truth of the matter is that much more focus was placed on front-end technologies than back end ones.  Additionally, the barrier to entry for back-end jobs is higher.  Thus, while I do think I have the capacity to make a great back-end developer, it will have to be later, rather than sooner.

After making that decision, it quickly became clear where I'll be spending my programming hours over the coming days and weeks.  Whether through building on an existing project or by starting something new, I'm going to do my darnedest to learn React.js.  It's a pretty hot technology that I enjoy using, and even if the job I get doesn't use it, specifically, it will likely use something very similar.


So, there it is!  I now know what my niche is and am learning to embrace it.  Instead of trying to be good at everything, I will try to be great at a few things.  Here's hoping that strategy will land me a job where I can prove my worth.
