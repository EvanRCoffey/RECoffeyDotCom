March 12th, 2013 - The Journey Begins

A Man, a Computer, a Desk, a Dream...

Hello, and welcome to my blog!

The purpose of this blog is to chronicle my journey as a young professional trying to find his place in the world.  Before I get started, allow me to tell you about how I got to where I am and where I want to go from here...

In 2004, when I graduated high school, I enrolled in a university, majoring in Instrumental Music Education.  I made good grades, I did good work, and I never had reason to doubt that what I was doing was right for me.

Then, in 2009, I was fortunate enough to be offered a teaching assistantship at a graduate school with one of the top Music Theory programs in the country.  I felt like I was seeing the benefits of doing well up to that point, and I tackled the challenge of graduate school head-on.

Unfortunately, after a semester and a half of hard work, I began to realize that music was not the career for me.  The love I originally had for it was disappearing; I felt like the more I learned about it, the less I cared.  So, I dropped out.  It wasn't an easy decision, but it was the right decision.

Since then, I've been on what some might call a "journey of self-discovery".  I've moved several times, held several different jobs, and even tried my hand at a few other large-scale creative projects to fill the void that music used to.

However, none of the places I've lived, none of the jobs I've held, and none of the creative projects I've completed have gotten me any closer to the lifestyle that I wanted for myself at this age (26).  I can no longer hide from the fact that my "journey of self-discovery" must end, and a new journey must begin - a journey that I hope will lead me toward a new career, a new set of interests, and a new life.  It is my hope that this blog will chronicle that journey in a way that may be fun, interesting, or helpful for others to read.

So, what am I, now, then?  Am I a musician?  No.  Am I a success?  No.  Am I what I wanted to be? No.  I am but a man.  A man with nothing more than a computer, a desk, and a dream...
